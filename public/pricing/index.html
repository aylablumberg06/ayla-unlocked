The call

Move it to $79. Today.

Your course is worth a lot more than $39. Here's why $79 is the right number:

· Market comps: Similar AI-for-beginners courses from Dickie Bush, Justin Welsh, Ali Abdaal, and Jay Clouse all sit $99–$297. The only ones cheaper than $50 are throwaways.

· Content density: 29 lessons + 14 exercises + 40 prompts + a real-chats archive + a live Ask-Ayla widget + a personalized certificate. You're well into "real course" territory.

· Who buys at $39 vs $79: $39 attracts casual "cool I'll check it out" buyers. $79 attracts people who will actually do the course and post about it. That second group becomes your marketing. The first group lurks.

· The math: even if raising to $79 drops conversion by 40%, you still net 22% more revenue per visitor. Pricing almost always wins this tradeoff.

· Your story premium: 19, self-taught, one month in, building agent teams for companies. That narrative is worth $40 alone. Don't underprice yourself for people who want to dismiss you.

Ship it at $79. Run a limited “founder's price” at $39 for the first 50 buyers as social proof, then flip it.

Stripe fee assumed at 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction, standard US. Doesn't factor in refunds, taxes, or marketing cost. Numbers are pre-tax gross.
